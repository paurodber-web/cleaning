How Much Does House Cleaning Cost In Melbourne? (2026 Pricing Guide)

If you're comparing cleaners in Melbourne, the hard part is not finding a price. It is figuring out what that price actually means. Some companies advertise hourly rates, others quote by home size, and many leave out extras that only appear once you are already halfway through the booking process.

This guide breaks down how house cleaning prices usually work in Melbourne, what affects the total, and when it makes more sense to book regular standard cleaning versus a deeper one-off clean. If you're pricing before booking, this is the information that matters.

How Much Does House Cleaning Cost In Melbourne?

For most homes, regular house cleaning in Melbourne starts from around $130 to $170 for smaller properties and rises with the size of the home, number of bathrooms, and level of detail required. Larger 3 to 4 bedroom homes often sit in the $180 to $260+ range for standard maintenance cleaning.

The quickest way to think about pricing is this: regular cleans are usually priced lower because the home is maintained, while one-off or neglected homes cost more because they need catch-up work.

Service Type Typical Pricing Style Estimated Range
Hourly cleaning Per cleaner / per hour Varies by provider
1 bedroom or studio Fixed price From $130 to $170
2 bedroom home Fixed price Around $150 to $210
3 to 4 bedroom home Fixed price Around $180 to $260+

These are practical guide ranges, not one-size-fits-all promises. Final pricing still depends on how often the home is cleaned, how many bathrooms are involved, and whether the job includes extras beyond a standard reset.

House Cleaning Prices By Home Size

Most homeowners and renters do not want abstract pricing theory. They want to know what a cleaner will likely cost for a home that looks like theirs. That is why fixed-price packages by property size are so common.

1 Bedroom Or Studio Apartment

Smaller homes are usually the most affordable because there is less floor area, fewer surfaces, and often only one bathroom. For routine maintenance cleaning, this is where you typically see the lower end of the price spectrum.

2 Bedroom Home

This is one of the most common price points in Melbourne. A 2-bedroom home often moves into the mid-range because there is usually a second sleeping area, more furniture, and sometimes an extra bathroom or powder room. If the home is cleaned regularly, pricing tends to stay more predictable.

3 To 4 Bedroom House

Once you move into family-home territory, time increases quickly. More bedrooms usually means more floors, more dusting, more bathrooms, and more lived-in surfaces. This is also where frequency makes a bigger difference. A weekly or fortnightly service may stay manageable, while a one-off monthly clean often takes noticeably longer.

Hourly Rate Vs. Fixed Price: Which Is Better?

Both models exist for a reason. The best option depends on the type of job, how predictable the scope is, and whether you care more about flexibility or certainty.

When Hourly Rates Make Sense

Hourly pricing can work well when the scope is unclear. Maybe you only want help with kitchens and bathrooms, or maybe you want a cleaner to work through a priority list rather than complete a full-home service. In those situations, hourly charging can feel more flexible.

Why Fixed-Price Packages Usually Suit House Cleaning Better

For most regular home cleaning, fixed pricing is easier to understand and easier to budget for. You know the cost before the booking starts, which makes it simpler to compare providers fairly. It also avoids the awkward uncertainty of watching the clock during a visit. For households booking recurring standard cleaning, fixed-price packages are usually the cleaner, more transparent option.

What Affects The Cost Of House Cleaning?

Two homes can have the same number of bedrooms and still receive very different quotes. That is because pricing is shaped by more than just floorplan.

Frequency Of Cleaning

Weekly and fortnightly cleans are generally better value than monthly or one-off visits. When a cleaner is maintaining a home consistently, there is less buildup to deal with each time. That makes the visit more efficient and usually reduces cost per appointment.

Condition Of The Property

This is one of the biggest pricing variables. A tidy, well-kept home is faster to clean than a home that has not had attention in weeks or months. Kitchens with grease buildup, bathrooms with soap scum, and dusty skirting or blinds all increase the time required.

Bathrooms, Kitchens, And Detail Work

Bathrooms and kitchens are usually the most time-intensive zones in a regular clean. A home with more wet areas or a kitchen that needs more detail will naturally cost more than a home with the same bedroom count but fewer high-effort spaces.

Location Within Melbourne

Travel, parking, access, and service area logistics can all affect pricing at the margins. A straightforward home with easy access is simpler to service than a property with difficult parking, limited lift access, or a longer travel window.

Optional Extras

Oven cleaning, inside windows, balcony wash-downs, and similar add-ons are usually not part of a standard regular clean. If you need these, they should be quoted separately so you know exactly what is included.

Standard Vs. Deep Clean Vs. End Of Lease: Price Comparison

One reason people get confused about cleaning prices is that not all cleaning services are the same product. A regular maintenance clean, a one-off deep clean, and an end of lease clean have completely different scopes.

Cleaning Type Best For Relative Cost
Standard Cleaning Weekly, fortnightly, or routine upkeep Lowest
Deep Cleaning One-off reset, neglected homes, seasonal refresh Higher
End Of Lease Cleaning Move-outs, inspections, bond return preparation Highest

If you are only trying to maintain your home week to week, a regular clean is usually the most sensible option. If the property needs a full reset first, a deep clean may be the better starting point before moving onto recurring maintenance.

Frequently Asked Questions About House Cleaning Costs

How Much Does A Cleaner Cost Per Hour In Melbourne?

Hourly rates vary by provider, team structure, and scope. Many clients prefer fixed pricing for regular house cleaning because it is easier to compare and budget for than open-ended hourly billing.

Is Weekly Cleaning Cheaper Than A One-Off Clean?

Usually yes. Weekly or fortnightly cleaning tends to be better value because the home stays maintained and requires less catch-up work each visit.

What Is Usually Included In A Standard House Clean?

A standard clean typically includes dusting, vacuuming, mopping, bathroom cleaning, and kitchen surface wiping. Exact inclusions vary, which is why it is important to compare scope as well as price.

When Should I Book A Deep Clean Instead?

If the home has not been professionally cleaned for a while, or if it needs detail work beyond routine maintenance, a deep clean is often the better starting point. It resets the property to a higher standard before ongoing regular visits.

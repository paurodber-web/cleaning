End Of Lease Cleaning Cost Melbourne: What You'll Actually Pay In 2026

If you're moving out of a rental in Melbourne, one of the first things on your mind is the bond. Getting it back in full comes down to one thing: leaving the property in the same condition it was when you moved in. That's where a professional end of lease clean comes in.

But how much should you actually expect to pay? This guide breaks it all down by property size, what's included, and what extras cost, so there are no surprises when you get your quote.

How Much Does End Of Lease Cleaning Cost In Melbourne?

End of lease cleaning in Melbourne is priced based on the size of the property, its condition, and any optional add-ons you need. Unlike standard regular cleaning, this is a comprehensive service that covers every part of the home to meet real estate agent inspection standards.

Here's a quick summary of what you can expect to pay:

Property Size Estimated Cost Approx. Time
Studio / 1 Bedroom From $410 3-4 hours
2 Bedroom $440 - $480 4-5 hours
3 Bedroom $520 - $580 5 - 7 hours
4+ Bedroom $620+ 7 - 9 hours

These figures reflect a professional standard clean that meets real estate agent inspection requirements. Properties that haven't been cleaned recently or are in poor condition may require additional time, which can affect the final price.

Why End Of Lease Cleaning Costs More Than A Regular Clean

A standard fortnightly or weekly clean keeps a home maintained. An end of lease clean is a completely different scope of work. Cleaners are required to bring every part of the property to a near-showroom standard. That means inside ovens, inside cupboards, exhaust fans, window tracks, laundry areas and everything in between. This level of detail takes significantly more time and expertise than a regular visit.

Real estate agents perform detailed inspections using checklists, and anything missed can result in a failed inspection and a bond deduction. A proper bond clean is essentially an insurance policy for your bond.

What's Included In A Professional End Of Lease Clean

A professional end of lease cleaning service should cover every room in the property. Here's what you can expect to be included as standard:

Kitchen

  • Stovetop and rangehood cleaning (exterior and underside)
  • Inside and outside of microwave
  • Wipe all bench tops and splashbacks
  • Clean and polish tapware and sink
  • Outside of all cupboards and appliances
  • Inside oven (included as standard in end of lease packages)
  • Inside all drawers and cabinets
  • Clean inside dishwasher
  • Vacuum and mop floors

Bathrooms

  • Scrub and disinfect shower, bath, and toilet
  • Clean all sinks and polish tapware
  • Wipe down all benches and mirrors
  • Inside all drawers and cabinets
  • Clean ceiling exhaust fans
  • Vacuum and mop floors

All Rooms & Common Areas

  • Wipe skirting boards, window sills, and door frames
  • Dust and wipe all reachable surfaces
  • Cobweb removal throughout
  • Clean light switches and power points
  • Dust and wipe blinds
  • Vacuum under beds and furniture
  • Vacuum and mop all floors

This is the standard inclusions list. If something was present and in good condition during your move-in inspection, it needs to be in the same condition when you leave.

Carpet steam cleaning is one of the most commonly requested add-ons. Many leases specifically require carpets to be professionally steam cleaned before vacating. If yours does, budget an additional $55 per room on top of your base clean cost.

What Affects The Final Price

The prices above give you a solid benchmark, but a few things can push the cost higher depending on your specific property.

Property Condition

A property that has been kept on top of throughout the tenancy will take less time to clean at the end. One that hasn't had a professional service in months, or where grease and grime has built up over time, will take more effort to bring up to inspection standard.

Number Of Bathrooms

Properties with two or more bathrooms take considerably longer to clean compared to single-bathroom homes of the same bedroom count. If your 3-bedroom home has two full bathrooms, expect the price to sit toward the higher end of the range.

Lease Agreement Requirements

Some leases specify that certain services, such as professional carpet steam cleaning or blind cleaning, must be carried out by a licensed technician. Always review your lease agreement before booking so you can include all required add-ons in a single visit.

Property Size And Layout

Open-plan homes with large living and dining areas often take longer than a compact apartment with the same number of bedrooms. Total floor area and the number of rooms will always play a role in the final time and cost.

DIY Vs. Professional End Of Lease Cleaning: Is It Worth It?

Many tenants try to clean the property themselves to save money. In practice, this often ends up costing more. Here's why.

The Risk Of A Failed Inspection

Real estate agents use detailed checklists and know exactly what to look for. Areas commonly missed in a DIY clean include inside exhaust fans, inside oven cavities, skirting boards, window tracks, and the underside of range hoods. A failed inspection means you'll need to arrange another clean anyway, and you risk losing hundreds of dollars from your bond in the meantime.

Your Time Has Value

A thorough end of lease clean on a 2-bedroom property typically takes 6 to 10 hours when done without professional equipment. Most tenants are also managing the stress of moving during this time, which makes a rushed or incomplete clean even more costly. A professional team completes the job efficiently, with the right tools and products, and stands behind their work.

Bond-Back Guarantee

A professional cleaning company that offers a bond-back guarantee will return to re-clean at no extra cost if the agent raises any issues after the inspection. That level of protection simply isn't available when you clean the property yourself. At Maid at Home, this guarantee is included with every end of lease clean.

What Real Estate Agents Actually Check

Understanding what property managers inspect helps you see exactly why a professional clean is worth the investment. The most commonly flagged areas during Melbourne rental inspections include:

  • Inside the oven, including racks and the oven door seal
  • Shower screens and shower floors for soap scum and limescale
  • Window tracks and fly screens
  • Inside cupboards and drawers (especially kitchens and bathrooms)
  • Ceiling exhaust fans
  • Skirting boards and door frames
  • Carpet condition (and whether steam cleaning was conducted if required)

These are the areas where tenants most commonly lose bond money. A professional service with experience in Melbourne bond cleans will address every one of these proactively.

How To Get The Most Out Of Your End Of Lease Clean

A few simple things on your end will help the clean go smoothly and make sure nothing gets missed.

  • Have the property clear of furniture so cleaners can access every area without obstruction.
  • Book early, particularly around the end of the month when demand is highest across Melbourne.
  • Check your lease and confirm any specific requirements like carpet steam cleaning or blind cleaning before the booking.
  • Make sure access is sorted so cleaners can get into every room, including the laundry, storage areas, and garage if needed.

If you're not sure exactly what your lease requires, get in touch with us and we can help you work through it before you book.

Frequently Asked Questions About End Of Lease Cleaning Costs

How Much Does End Of Lease Cleaning Cost For A 2-Bedroom Apartment In Melbourne?

A professional end of lease clean for a 2-bedroom apartment in Melbourne typically costs between $440 and $480. This includes all standard inclusions such as inside cupboards, oven, bathrooms, and all floors. Optional extras such as carpet steam cleaning and inside windows are priced separately.

Is End Of Lease Cleaning Tax Deductible?

For investment property owners, end of lease cleaning is generally tax deductible as a property maintenance expense. For residential tenants, it is not. Always consult a tax professional to confirm your specific situation.

How Long Does A Professional End Of Lease Clean Take?

Depending on the property size and condition, a professional end of lease clean takes between 3 and 9 hours. A studio or 1-bedroom typically takes 3-4 hours. A 3-4 bedroom home with multiple bathrooms and add-ons can take 7-9 hours or more.

Does End Of Lease Cleaning Include Carpet Steam Cleaning?

Carpet steam cleaning is not automatically included in the standard end of lease package. It is available as an optional add-on at $55 per room. If your lease requires professional carpet cleaning, add this to your booking.

What If The Property Fails The Final Inspection?

If any issues are raised by the property manager after the clean, we return and rectify them at no additional cost. This bond-back guarantee gives you confidence that the job will be done to the required standard, no matter what.

End of Lease Cleaning Melbourne: The Ultimate Bond Back Guide & Checklist

In this guide:

  • 1. The Real Cost of DIY Vacate Cleaning
  • 2. Kitchen & Appliances Checklist
  • 3. Bathroom & Laundry Deep Scrub
  • 4. Walls, Windows & Floors
  • 5. Mandatory Carpet Steam Cleaning
  • 6. How to Guarantee Your Bond Return

Moving out of a Melbourne rental property is notoriously stressful. Between packing boxes and organizing transport, the last thing you want is a dispute over your security deposit. Securing your full bond return heavily depends on the final condition of the property. This is why a professional end of lease clean is your best investment. Real estate agents use a strict bond cleaning checklist in Melbourne to inspect every inch of your home. If a single ceiling fan is dusty, they may deduct from your bond.

1. The Real Cost of DIY Vacate Cleaning

Unlike standard weekly upkeep, an end of lease cleaning in Melbourne is an intensive, top-to-bottom reset. Property managers expect the residence to look exactly as it did the day you moved in. Attempting a DIY vacate clean usually takes 15 to 20 hours for a standard apartment. Without professional-grade degreasers and equipment, you risk leaving microscopic residue that agents will spot immediately.

2. Kitchen & Appliances Checklist

The kitchen is the number one reason tenants lose their bond. A proper end of lease cleaning checklist for Melbourne renters must include:

  • Complete oven cleaning, including racks, glass doors, and trays.
  • Degreasing the rangehood, filters, and exhaust fans.
  • Wiping inside and outside of all cupboards, pantries, and drawers.
  • Sanitizing sinks, polishing tapware, and cleaning the dishwasher filter.

3. Bathroom & Laundry Deep Scrub

Real estate agents look for mould, calcium buildup, and hidden hair. Securing your bond requires removing ingrained soap scum from shower glass, scrubbing grout lines, sanitizing toilets (behind and under), and polishing mirrors until zero streaks remain. Exhaust fans must be completely dust-free.

4. Walls, Windows & Floors

Skirting boards often collect thick layers of dust that are easily overlooked. You must spot-clean walls for scuff marks, thoroughly wash windows (inside), wipe down window sills, and vacuum window tracks. Hard floors require a heavy-duty mop to remove sticky residues left hiding under furniture.

5. Mandatory Carpet Steam Cleaning

If your rental has carpets, standard vacuuming won't pass the final inspection. Over 90% of Melbourne tenancy agreements specify that professional carpet steam cleaning must be performed upon moving out, and you must present a valid receipt to the property manager. Our high-pressure hot water extraction removes deep-seated allergens and stubborn stains.

6. How to Guarantee Your Bond Return

Why risk your bond money to save a few hours? At Maid at Home, our move-out specialists strictly follow comprehensive, industry-standard checklists. We know exactly what property managers look for, and if they flag a cleaning issue during the final inspection, we offer an industry-leading 100% Bond Back Guarantee. This means we will return to fix it at absolutely no extra cost to you.
